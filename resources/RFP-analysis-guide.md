# RFP Analysis Guide

A structured approach to reading and analysing a Request for Proposals. Work through these questions in order. Write down your answers — they become the foundation for your proposal.

---

## 1. What is the funder's core problem?

Read the Introduction and Background section. In one sentence, what problem is the funder trying to solve? Don't paraphrase the RFP — restate it in your own words.

## 10. How can data strengthen this response?

The RFP asks you to describe how you would use data. But there are two ways to respond:

- **Option A:** Describe your data approach — "We will use geospatial data to identify vulnerable communities."
- **Option B:** Demonstrate your data approach — back up your proposal with a preliminary analysis that shows the methodology working.

Which is more convincing to a funder? What would a preliminary analysis look like for this RFP?

## 11. What is your angle?

You can't respond to everything in the RFP with equal depth. What specific angle will your proposal take?

Consider:
- A specific **region** or country
- A specific **hazard type** (floods, drought, extreme heat)
- A specific **population** (elderly, children under 5, displaced communities)
- A specific **infrastructure gap** (health facilities, shelters, transport)
